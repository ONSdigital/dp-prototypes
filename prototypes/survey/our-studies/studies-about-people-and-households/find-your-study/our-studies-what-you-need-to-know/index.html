1. About us and our studies

Our studies build a picture of life in Great Britain today. We produce statistics on our society and economy by asking members of the public questions on a range of topics. To make sure our statistics represent everyone, we invite people from all walks of life to take part.

Statistics from our studies help shape society. Government departments, local authorities, businesses, charities, academics and others use our statistics daily to understand society and make important decisions based on evidence.

One of our most important studies is the census. We carry this out every 10 years to gather information about the whole population in England and Wales. Between each census we rely on our studies to provide up-to-date information about changes to society and to give an accurate picture of life today.

2. Why have I been selected?

To build a picture of our whole society, we need to talk to all types of people.

We carry out the census every 10 years to gather information about the whole population in England and Wales. Between each census we rely on ongoing studies to provide up-to-date information about changes to society. It’s not possible to ask everyone to take part in our ongoing studies – instead, we invite a sample of individuals or households which makes sure we represent everyone.

Whoever you are, whatever you do, we want to hear from you.

3. How we’ve selected you

The way we select people to take part can vary based on the study. Many of our studies use the Royal Mail’s list of addresses to select you or your address at random.

You can find out more about the way you were selected for your study under the individual study pages.

If you or your household are selected, we cannot ask anyone else to take your place as this would affect how representative our studies are.

4. Why should I take part?

This is your unique opportunity to tell us about your opinions and experiences. Government departments, public bodies and charities use the findings from our studies to make plans and to meet the country’s changing needs. These range from national policies to facilities and services local to you. To help inform decision-making, we must build an accurate picture of our diverse and changing population. Asking you questions allows us to gather the range of information to produce the statistics needed to help shape tomorrow.

Your responses will provide information not available from any other sources. Don’t miss your chance to have your say.

5. What’s next?

You should have received a letter inviting you to take part. This introduces the study and includes information about whether we’d like you to take part over the phone, face-to-face or online. Your letter tells you whether the study involves everyone in the household, or if we only need to hear from the person the letter is addressed to.

Taking part face-to-face or on the phone?

Once you’ve received this letter, you can arrange your interview for a time that suits you. If we don’t hear from you, one of our security-cleared interviewers will be in touch to arrange an appointment for you to take part.

Taking part online?

For our online studies, your letter will include all the instructions and login details you will need – these studies are available for you to complete at any time until the closing date stated in your letter. You can find out more about how to complete the study we’ve invited you to take part in under the individual study pages.

We design our studies with you in mind and keep them as short as possible. We only collect the information we need to better understand our society. Your response is really important to us. We know everyone is busy, so if your plans change and it’s no longer a good time, we’re happy for you to rearrange. Once you’ve completed the study, we will combine the answers you give us with everybody else’s. We then use this data to produce statistics.

6. What will I be asked?

What we ask you will be different for each study. There are no difficult questions and we have made sure you don’t need any specialist knowledge to take part.

Our studies cover a range of topics including:

  • work
  • health
  • retirement
  • education
  • unemployment
  • looking after the family or home
  • spending
  • housing
  • experiences of crime

The questions we ask can be based on both facts and opinions.

There are no right or wrong answers – your honest responses are vital for us to produce reliable statistics about our whole society.

We respect your right to a private life – the information you provide in our studies will not be used to identify or incriminate you in any way. If you’re uncomfortable with any of the questions you’re asked, you can always leave them out.

7. What will you do with my answers?

Your personal information will remain confidential.

The details you provide in your interview will be combined with those of everyone else taking part so we can produce statistics. Any data we publish will never identify you or your household.

We do not sell on your data and you will not receive junk mail or marketing calls as a result of taking part in one of our studies.

If you want to know more, please see our section about our commitment to protecting your data.

8. Who is carrying out the study?

Most of the studies we run collect information which we then use to produce statistics. Some of our studies involve one of our security-cleared interviewers coming to your home to carry out the study in person. Others will be completed by yourselves on paper, over the telephone, or online. The Office for National Statistics (ONS) is the country’s largest producer of statistics

We are:

✔ independent, unbiased and impartial. We are separate from other organisations and do not allow anything to influence the statistics we publish.

✔ the only organisation that produces official statistics as a primary role. We have no additional interest in the information we collect.

✔ only interested in society as a whole, and not you as an individual. Statistics represent groups of people. We remove your personal details as we aren’t interested in singling you out.

We are not:

✘ a commercial or market research organisation. We do not work for profit and won’t try to sell you anything.

✘ associated with any political parties. We produce official statistics, no matter who the prime minister or political party in government is.

✘ going to sell on your data under any circumstances? We value your involvement in our studies and do not profit from your data. You will not receive any ‘junk mail’ as a result of taking part.

✘ monitoring you, the data you give is only used for producing statistics. We will not use your information to contact you about other matters such as your tax, income or benefits, and we will not provide your information to anyone else for them to do so either.

Read more about what we do at ONS.

In most cases, interviews will be carried out by ONS interviewers. All of our interviewers are security cleared, and they will always carry an identification card, which includes their photograph and interviewer identification number.

Working with other organisations

At times we may collect information for a study on behalf of another organisation. In these cases, the information is provided to the organisation for them to use in their own statistical production, for example in the National Survey for Wales.

On other occasions, another research organisation may collect information on our behalf. They will provide us with the data so we can produce statistics. We care about you - any interviewers from other organisations we work with are security-cleared, like our own.

We always make sure that organisations we work with treat your data responsibly. For more information please read about our commitment to you.

To find out about who is conducting the study, and using the information collected, please find your study to read about the study that you’ve been selected for.

9. ONS’s commitment to you and how we treat your information

What you can expect from us

You can read about the commitments ONS makes to those taking part in its studies on our Taking part? What you can expect from us page.

How we treat your information – confidentiality and data protection

UK legislation allows us to collect and process your data to produce statistics for the public good. Your information will be treated as confidential as directed by the Code of Practice for Statistics. It will only be held for as long as it is being used for producing statistics. The statistics produced will not identify you or anyone in your household.

Survey information is also provided to other government departments, approved organisations and approved researchers for statistical purposes only. Details on who can access this information can be found on the approved organisations and government departments page and the approved researchers page. All statistics produced will be subject to the Code and the same standards of protection will be applied to your data at all times. If you have a question about how we process your personal data or want to find out more about your rights under data protection legislation, please see our data protection page for more information and the contact details you will need.

When working with other organisations, we always make sure that they look after your data. For more information about how your data will be treated in these circumstances, please go to the Find your study page to read about the survey that you’ve been selected for.

At ONS, we take seriously our responsibility to the public. It is our policy that we will tell the appropriate authority if we believe there is a serious risk to someone's safety.

10. Do I have to take part?

We hope we’ve explained just how important your response is to us, and the value of your information in producing reliable statistics for decision-making. If you missed this, or need a refresher, see our section on ‘Why should I take part?’

It’s your chance to have your voice heard. If you’re still not convinced, you don’t have to take part if you don’t want to. For us to paint an accurate picture of our society, it is vital that we hear from as many people as possible, from all walks of life.

In many other countries it is compulsory for selected individuals to take part. We are giving you the choice to make a difference.
