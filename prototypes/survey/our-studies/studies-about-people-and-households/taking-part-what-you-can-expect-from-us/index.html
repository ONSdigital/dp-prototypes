Taking part? What you can expect from us

Explains what you can expect from us if you are asked to take part in one of our studies, or a study we are carrying for another organisation.

We value your time and participation

  • We design our studies to take as little of your time as possible.
  • We make sure each study is necessary and that the information is not available anywhere else.
  • We will always try to arrange a time that’s convenient for you and advise how long the study will take
  • We don’t select the same address for more than one study in the same year.
  • By taking part, you make sure people like you are represented in statistics - thank you.

We will treat you with respect

  • Your participation is voluntary and you have the choice to skip any question.
  • We will respect your privacy at your home, on the phone, in a port or airport and online.
  • We set high standards for our staff, through recruitment, training and management practices.
  • We work hard to design our studies so they are accessible to everybody.

We value your trust

  • We will identify ourselves from the Office for National Statistics (ONS) whenever we contact you whenever we contact you
  • Our logo will be displayed on all our communication with you
  • Our interviewers wear photo ID cards and have been vetted, if you wish to verify their identity you can call ONS for free on 0800 298 5313
  • We will never ask for your bank details, national insurance number or any other identification numbers

We will keep your information secure and confidential

  • We ensure our staff are aware of their obligations to protect your confidential information
  • We maintain the security of our systems and buildings
  • We recognise that you are sharing personal information with us so we will always uphold our confidentiality pledge:

UK legislation allows us to collect and process your data to produce statistics for the public good.

Your information will be treated as confidential as directed by the Code of Practice for Statistics. It will only be held for as long as it is being used for producing statistics. The statistics produced will not identify you or anyone in your household.

Survey information is also provided to other government departments, approved organisations and approved researchers for statistical purposes only. Details on who can access this information can be found on the approved organisations and government departments page and the approved researchers page. All statistics produced will be subject to the Code and the same standards of protection will be applied to your data at all times.

If you have a question about how we process your personal data or want to find out more about your rights under data protection legislation, please see our data protection page.

We will communicate with you and listen to your views

  • Our study pages will help you understand the background to the study and explain what taking part involves. These are listed on the Find your study page.
  • We have a freephone line where our staff will be able to answer any of your questions
  • We will explain how you have been selected to take part, what the study is about and how the results are used.
  • We welcome your views on any of our studies, this commitment or your experience of taking part - visit our contact details page to get in touch.

Working with other organisations

Sometimes we collect information on behalf of another organisation. For more information about how your data will be treated in these circumstances, please go to the Find your study page to read about the study that you’ve been selected for. Sometimes other research organisations carry out studies for us: the Crime Survey for England and Wales is currently being carried out by Kantar Public. To find out more about Kantar Public’s commitment please go to the Kantar Public website.
